## Key Strategic Questions for SK Hynix Senior Management

1. **HBM Capacity Race**: Micron ramping HBM production 2025-26 and will reach 15-20% market share by 2027. Will this pressure HBM pricing and margins? Should we accelerate M16 and M17 fab expansions to maintain 60%+ share?

2. **NVIDIA Diversification Risk**: NVIDIA wants to reduce dependence on SK Hynix by qualifying Micron and Samsung as secondary HBM suppliers. How do we maintain primary supplier status and prevent margin erosion from multi-sourcing?

3. **US Manufacturing Imperative**: Micron's $6.1B CHIPS Act funding and US fab advantage secures government/defense deals and political favor. Should SK Hynix accelerate Indiana packaging facility and lobby for more subsidies?

4. **NAND Strategy**: Micron committed to NAND (40% of revenue) despite low margins. SK Hynix reducing NAND focus to prioritize HBM/DRAM. Is this the right strategy, or do we lose bundling opportunities with hyperscalers?

5. **Technology Roadmap**: Can we maintain 6-12 month HBM technology lead through HBM4 generation (2026-27)? What R&D and CapEx investments are required? What happens if Micron closes the gap?

6. **China Market**: Both companies face China risks‚ÄîMicron banned from some segments, SK Hynix has geopolitical exposure. Does Micron's US positioning create competitive disadvantage for us in non-China markets?

7. **Customer Concentration**: SK Hynix is heavily exposed to NVIDIA for HBM (60%+ of HBM revenue). Micron's diversified customer base is less risky. Should we aggressively pursue AMD, Intel, hyperscaler direct deals to reduce NVIDIA dependency?
